Dynamic Zoom and Layout Impacts

Content Visuals

Non-decorative Visuals (i.e. Content Visuals) that have an essential second dimension to be valuable, can have an additional scroll bar specific to its container.

Keyboard Users who zoom

Keyboard users need access to the scroll bar the content visuals generate on zoom such that. . .

  • they can scroll using the keyboard
  • they do not get lost as they move from the scrolling content to non scrolling content.

Users who are blind

A screen reader user will be informed that a Content Visual exists in the layout along with its associated alt-text.

However it is unclear, how adding a tabindex for the Content Visual (because of the keyboard user who zooms) 'works' for a screen reader users.

Keep the User informed

Consistent with usability for users who are not blind, keeping the user informed throughout an experience is integral to a good UX. With this in mind. . .

  • Mirror the experience of other interactive semantic elements like a (links), or button elements.
  • Do not implement ARIA attributes that are inconsistent with the roles that a img element typically has.
  • Test your implementation with an actual user who is blind.
